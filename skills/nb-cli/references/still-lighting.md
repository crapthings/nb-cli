# Still Lighting Language

Use this file when a still image needs clear lighting vocabulary.

Ignore techniques whose meaning mainly depends on movement or sequence progression. Keep the language usable in one frame.

## Core setups
- `three-point lighting`: polished key, fill, and backlight setup.
- `key light`: main shaping light.
- `fill light`: softens shadows and lowers contrast.
- `backlight` / `rim light` / `hair light`: separate subject from background.
- `accent light`: emphasize one detail or prop.
- `motivated lighting`: believable source direction from windows, lamps, or signs.
- `practical lighting`: visible in-scene lamps, candles, or neon contribute to the image.

## Light quality and direction
- `soft light`: gentle shadows, flattering surfaces.
- `hard light`: sharp shadows, crisp texture.
- `side lighting`: emphasize shape and texture.
- `top lighting`: overhead dramatic illumination.
- `frontal lighting`: even, flat, beauty-oriented illumination.
- `bounce light`: soft reflected fill.
- `diffused light`: softened source with less harsh contrast.
- `split lighting`: half-lit face for strong drama.
- `loop lighting`: flattering portrait-lighting pattern.
- `Rembrandt lighting`: portrait setup with a lit triangle on the shadow side.
- `butterfly lighting`: high centered glamour portrait light.

## Mood and environment
- `high-key lighting`: bright, low-contrast, clean, optimistic.
- `low-key lighting`: dark, contrasty, moody.
- `chiaroscuro`: bold sculptural light-dark contrast.
- `golden hour`: warm soft sunlight.
- `blue hour`: cool twilight atmosphere.
- `silhouette`: dark subject outline against a brighter background.
- `firelight`: warm flickering orange glow.
- `neon lighting`: saturated colored urban light.
- `volumetric lighting`: visible beams through haze or fog.
- `dappled light`: patterned light through leaves, blinds, or cutouts.
- `gobo` / `cucoloris`: cast patterned shadows for window, branch, or blind effects.

## Prompting guidance
- Use one setup term plus one quality term plus one mood term when possible.
- For products, ask for accurate color and controlled reflections when needed.
- For portraits, pick one portrait-lighting pattern rather than listing several.
