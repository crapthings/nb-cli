# Still Composition Language

Use this file when a still image needs layout, hierarchy, or focus language.

Ignore editing and sequencing concepts. This file is only for one-frame composition.

## Layout and balance
- `rule of thirds`: place focal elements on thirds lines or intersections.
- `centered composition`: direct and formal.
- `symmetry`: mirrored, ordered, graphic balance.
- `balancing elements`: distribute visual weight across the frame.
- `triangular composition`: stable relation among three visual points.
- `negative space`: empty area to emphasize the subject or leave room for design.
- `headroom`: control space above the head.
- `lead room`: leave space in the direction a subject faces or looks.

## Depth and spatial design
- `leading lines`: guide the eye to the subject.
- `framing within the frame`: use windows, arches, branches, or doorways as a secondary frame.
- `foreground interest`: add front-layer depth.
- `figure-ground separation`: keep subject distinct from background using light, color, or focus.
- `visual layers`: foreground, midground, and background read clearly.

## Focus and clarity
- `shallow depth of field`: isolated subject, blurred background.
- `deep focus`: keep multiple depth planes sharp.
- `depth of field`: general note about focus range when the exact look is flexible.

## Shape and pattern
- `diagonal lines`: add tension and energy.
- `repetition and pattern`: create rhythm.
- `contrast`: guide attention via difference in value, color, size, or texture.
- `visual weight`: define what dominates the frame.

## Prompting guidance
- Use composition terms to improve readability, emphasis, and mood, not to decorate the prompt.
- For UI, infographic, and icon work, prioritize clarity and hierarchy over cinematic phrasing.
